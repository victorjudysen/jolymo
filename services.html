Complete Guide to Clearing & Forwarding

Understanding the import/export process from start to finish

Import Process: From Origin to Destination

Phase 1: Pre-Shipment Planning

Before goods leave the origin country, proper planning ensures smooth clearance. We work with your suppliers to ensure all documentation is prepared correctly:

  • Verify HS codes with supplier for accurate classification
  • Confirm Incoterms (FOB, CIF, DDP, etc.) to determine responsibilities
  • Arrange pre-shipment inspection if required (PVOC, CoC)
  • Obtain necessary permits (import license, TFDA for pharma)
  • Confirm buyer/importer details match shipping documents
  • Arrange cargo insurance coverage

Phase 2: International Transit

Once goods depart origin, we monitor the shipment and prepare for arrival:

  • Receive shipping documents (Bill of Lading, Airway Bill)
  • Track vessel/flight schedule for arrival date
  • Prepare customs declaration (Single Administrative Document)
  • Submit advance cargo information to customs
  • Pre-calculate duties and taxes for client approval
  • Coordinate with shipping line/airline for cargo release

üìã Required Documents for Import

Essential Documents:

  • Commercial Invoice (original + 3 copies)
  • Packing List detailing contents
  • Bill of Lading (B/L) or Air Waybill (AWB)
  • Certificate of Origin (preferential treatment)
  • Import Declaration Form (IDF)

Additional Documents (when applicable):

  • Import Permit from relevant ministry
  • TFDA permit (pharmaceuticals, cosmetics, food)
  • Phytosanitary certificate (plants, seeds)
  • Veterinary certificate (animal products)
  • Standards certificate (TBS, PVOC)
  • Insurance certificate
  • Fumigation certificate (wooden packaging)

Phase 3: Port/Airport Arrival

Upon arrival at Dar es Salaam port or airport, customs clearance begins:

  • Cargo manifest submitted by carrier to TRA
  • We verify cargo arrival and container/package status
  • Submit electronic customs declaration via TANCIS system
  • Pay port/airport handling charges and storage fees
  • Obtain delivery order from shipping line/airline
  • Await customs risk assessment (green, yellow, red channel)

Phase 4: Customs Assessment & Examination

TRA customs officers review the declaration and may conduct examination:

  • Green Channel: Automatic clearance, no inspection
  • Yellow Channel: Document verification only
  • Red Channel: Physical inspection required

For physical inspections, we coordinate container positioning, arrange stuffing/ destuffing if needed, and ensure customs officers can examine goods properly. Inspection typically takes 1-2 hours.

Phase 5: Duty Payment & Release

Once customs approves the declaration, duties must be paid:

  • Receive customs assessment notice with duty calculation
  • Client approves and provides payment authorization
  • Pay import duty (0-25% based on EAC CET)
  • Pay VAT (18% on CIF value + duty)
  • Pay excise duty (if applicable - alcohol, tobacco, vehicles)
  • Obtain customs release order (SAD stamped "Cleared")
  • Pay port demurrage if applicable

Phase 6: Cargo Collection & Delivery

Final stage is collecting cargo from port/airport and delivering to client:

  • Arrange truck/vehicle for cargo collection
  • Present release documents at port/airport gate
  • Load cargo under supervision
  • Conduct cargo inspection for damage (note exceptions)
  • Transport to client warehouse or designated location
  • Obtain delivery signature and POD
  • Provide client with all original documents

Export Process: From Tanzania to Regional Markets

Export Documentation Requirements

Exporting from Tanzania to Zimbabwe, Zambia, DRC, Rwanda, or Burundi requires:

  • Export Declaration Form (EDF) submitted to TRA
  • Commercial Invoice with buyer details
  • Packing List with cargo description
  • Certificate of Origin (Form CO or EUR.1 for preferential access)
  • Export permit (if controlled goods)
  • Quality certificate from TBS (if required)
  • Phytosanitary certificate (agricultural exports)

Export Clearance Process

  • Goods moved to port/border under transit bond
  • Export declaration submitted electronically
  • Customs may inspect for verification
  • Export clearance issued within 24 hours
  • Goods loaded on vessel/truck for export
  • Bill of Lading issued by carrier
  • Documents sent to buyer for import clearance

Understanding Duty & Tax Calculations

Import Duty Structure

Import duties are calculated based on the EAC Common External Tariff:

  • Raw materials: 0-10% duty rate
  • Intermediate goods: 10-25% duty rate
  • Finished consumer goods: 25% duty rate
  • Capital equipment: 0-10% duty rate

Calculation Formula

Total import tax = CIF Value √ó (Duty % + VAT % + Excise %)

Example: Importing machinery worth $10,000 CIF

  • CIF Value: $10,000
  • Import Duty (10%): $1,000
  • Dutiable Value: $11,000
  • VAT (18%): $1,980
  • Total Tax: $2,980

Preferential Duty Rates

Goods originating from EAC, COMESA, or SADC countries may qualify for reduced or zero duty rates with proper Certificate of Origin.
